How The Phone Network Works¶

Origin From: Scishow

The Basic Way A Cell Phone Works¶

Cell phones are basically just complicated radios, send radio waves carrying a signal back and forth between radio towers.

Complicated part

Encoding data on a radio wave.

Radio waves have two properties

  1. Amplitude: how large the wave is.
  2. Frequency: the number of times a wave cycles in a given tiem.

We can encoding information by changing both of those properties across a wave. (for instance, we could use a series of high and low frequencies to represent a stream of 1s and 0s, encoding digital data)

Cell phones use a slice of the radio frequency spectrum in 800 to 4000 Megahertz range.

Bandwidth

Bandwidth is the amount of frequencies we can use for a whole network.

Interface

If lots of cell phones all use the same band of frequencies in the same way to communicate with a cell tower, their signals overlap with each other and become garbled.

Radio waves can add and subtract from one another to create new waves.

Overview of 1G to 5G¶

The “gees”

The letter G in 4G or 5G refers to the generation of technology used for sending radio waves around.

1G¶

FDMA: frequency domain multiple access

Distributing frequencies in cells (a kind of honeycomb pattern), each of those cells uses a difference cell tower or base station to transmit to.

The trick is that nearby cells are allocated different frequencies to use, but distant ones can use the same frequencies without overlapping.

2G¶

TDMA: time division multiple access

Use the same channel by chunking signals into bits that take turns.

3G¶

CDMA: code division multiple access

Encoding the signals in such way that they can still be made out distinctly on the other end (the codes are orthogonal, meaning that they can’t get mixed up with one another).

4G¶

OFDM: orthoganal frequency division multiplexing

Chopes up a single band into small divisions called carriers whose frequencies are less likely to interface with each other.

ICI: Intercell Interference

Signals interfering at the edge of two cells using the same frequencies.

5G¶

5G is a network of what are called “ultra-dense networks” consisting of lots of smaller cells providing reception to many users.

The problem

More boundaries, increasing the amount of ICI, lead you cell phone drop reception very often.
